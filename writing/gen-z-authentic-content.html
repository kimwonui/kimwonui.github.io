Gen Z & Creators/4 min read/June 2026

Gen Z Can Smell a Fake Ad From Eight Seconds Away.

For Gen Z, authenticity outsells polish. The brands winning attention build creator-style series, not one-off ads.

Trust follows people, not logos

Gen Z trusts humans over brands. About 61% trust creator recommendations over brand ads, and roughly 68% say unpolished content beats highly produced ads, per New Media Campaigns.

That flips the old playbook. A shaky phone video from someone they believe can outperform a budget-heavy commercial. Production value isn’t the point — credibility is.

Think in episodes, not posts

The other shift is format. Creators now build episodic, recurring content ecosystems, as WPP Media notes — series people follow, not posts they scroll past.

Running IC, a Gen Z content studio, I plan in episodes. A recurring series earns a returning audience; a one-off ad earns a single impression and then silence.

So when a brand asks me for “a viral post,” I push back. Give people a reason to come back next week. With this audience, that’s what actually compounds.
