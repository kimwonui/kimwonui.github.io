Gen Z Can Smell a Fake Ad From Eight Seconds Away

Gen Z & Creators / Mar 2025

Authentic creator-style content beats polished ads every single time. Here's why that matters, and how to actually reach Gen Z.

The polished ad is dead

I grew up watching Super Bowl ads. Perfect lighting, perfect actors, perfect message. Everything was *crafted*.

Gen Z didn't grow up with that. They grew up with YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram — where the best content is often the most *imperfect*. A shaky phone recording of someone laughing. A video shot in a bedroom with bad lighting. A stream where the creator just talks, no script.

The moment an ad looks like an *ad*, Gen Z is already scrolling.

They've seen 10,000 ads today

We're all numb to advertising. You can't watch a five-minute video without interruption. Sponsored posts are everywhere. Every creator has brand deals.

So when a brand tries to sell something with perfect production value and polished messaging, it registers as *obvious advertising*. The brain sees it and filters it out automatically.

But when a creator you actually like casually mentions something they use? That's different. That feels like a recommendation from a friend.

Authenticity is the only currency that works

I lead a content studio with 30 people. We make videos, graphics, and campaigns. And the stuff that gets engagement is always the same: it's weird, it's honest, it's the kind of thing that feels like the creator *actually* cares.

One of our videos got 2,000 views. It was edited, scripted, perfect. Another video with no script, shot on an iPhone, got 8,000 views in the same timeframe. The difference? One felt real. The other felt like a *thing we made*.

Gen Z can tell when you're performing for them versus when you're just existing and inviting them in.

The algorithm rewards engagement, not perfection

TikTok doesn't care if your lighting is perfect. YouTube doesn't care if you have a Hollywood editor. Instagram doesn't care if you're wearing makeup.

They care about *watch time* and *engagement*. And what keeps people watching? Not a perfect ad. A moment of truth. A moment that makes them feel like they're in on something real.

The brands winning with Gen Z are the ones who stopped trying to look like ads and started trying to look like content worth sharing with friends.

What's actually happening underneath

Gen Z grew up with infinite content. You learn very quickly to distinguish signal from noise. Authenticity is *recognizable* — it stands out because it's rare.

When a brand is trying to sell you something, you feel it in the production. The color correction, the music choice, the perfectly timed laugh track. It all reads as *manipulation*.

But when someone is just sharing something they find useful or funny, it's obvious too. The production is sloppy because they don't care about production — they care about the idea.

How to actually reach Gen Z

Stop thinking like a marketer. Start thinking like a creator. What would you share with friends? That's the content that works.

Imperfection is a feature, not a bug. Bad lighting, weird angles, a moment where you laugh at your own joke — keep it. That's what makes it feel real.

Don't sell to them. Invite them. "Here's this thing, and if you use it too, we have something in common." That's the vibe that works.

Be weirdly specific. Don't make ads for "everyone." Make something for *your* people. The niche, specific audience who will actually care. They'll share it with their friends.

The IC creative team — where we learned to make content that actually resonates

The real shift

We're past the era of advertising as interruption. The brands winning now are the ones who became *part of the culture* instead of selling into it. They didn't become advertisers — they became creators.

And that's the only way to reach Gen Z: stop making ads. Make culture instead.
