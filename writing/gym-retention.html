What Gyms Taught Me About Keeping Customers, Not Just Getting Them.

Acquisition gets the spotlight, but retention pays the bills. The cheapest customer to win is the one you already have.

The leaky bucket

Gyms are a brutal lesson in churn. Fitness operators lose roughly a third of their members every 12 months, according to ABC Fitness. You can fill the top of the bucket all you want — it still leaks.

It gets worse early. Nearly half of new members quit within their first six months. Those opening weeks quietly decide whether someone stays a year or simply disappears.

Belonging beats discounts

Here’s the part marketers underuse: members with social ties at the gym are far less likely to leave. A workout buddy keeps people coming back better than any promo code.

I saw this firsthand researching competitor gyms for a Korean fitness brand. The places that held onto members weren’t the cheapest — they built community, routine, and a reason to return.

So I’d spend less on the next discount and more on the second visit: onboarding, check-ins, a familiar face at the door. Retention isn’t glamorous, but it compounds.
